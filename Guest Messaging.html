How to Approach Guests: Crafting the Perfect Message

Be Warm, Not Weird ü§ó

Use a friendly and professional tone. Use their name, add emojis where appropriate, and keep it positive. Avoid overly casual language, slang, or prying into personal details.

Be Concise & Clear üìù

Guests are often traveling and busy. Keep your messages short and to the point. Use formatting like bullet points or short paragraphs for important information like check-in instructions.

Personalize, Personalize! ‚ú®

Did they mention they're in town for a birthday? Wish them a happy birthday! Are they traveling with kids? Suggest a nearby park. This small effort makes guests feel seen and valued.

The Golden Rule of Contact

Always use the platform's messaging system for all communication. It protects both you and the guest, as there's a clear record of all conversations. Only use phone calls for genuine, time-sensitive emergencies.
